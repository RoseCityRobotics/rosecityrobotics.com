This summer, Portland State University is hosting a **completely free 3-week STEM camp** for rising 6th–8th graders as part of the **Verizon Innovative Learning STEM Achievers** initiative.

Students will dive into **design thinking, 3D printing, augmented reality, and social entrepreneurship** all in a supportive, tech-powered environment designed to close the opportunity gap and build future leaders in STEM.

💥 **Rose City Robotics is leading one of the hands-on days** for each session this summer with our signature _Intro to Robotics Workshop_.

🧠 Middle school students (grades 6–8)  
&nbsp;📍 Portland State University  
&nbsp;📆 Two Dates (RCR teaches one day per session, exact date TBA)

- **Session 1:** July 14 – August 1, 2025
- **Session 2:** August 4 – August 22, 2025

&nbsp;💸 Free to attend – registration is required, and space is limited  
&nbsp;🛠 No experience required

### About the Program

The **STEM Achievers** initiative is sponsored by Verizon and the National Association for Community College Entrepreneurship (NACCE). This program brings **project-based, tech-powered learning** to underserved middle schoolers across the country. With a focus on design thinking, 3D printing, augmented reality, and robotics, the program prepares students to explore future careers in STEM while building the confidence to lead.

At Rose City Robotics, we’re proud to contribute to this mission by introducing students to engineering concepts through mentorship, hands-on experimentation, and an equity-first approach to education.
